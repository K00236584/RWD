Expansion


The NFL is always looking to expand. The commision belives that the current 32 NFL team is getting stale.
To counteract this, they plan on expanding the NFL to a 40 league team. The problems are that most cities
that are big enough already have a NFL team or for some two. The NFL do like to play a few games in London
every year and some in Mexico City. Could these two cities get enough traction to hold interest for an entire
season? Would a owner want their team in these cities as chances are the goverments won't play the way that the
US city goverments do? For all these reasons is it smart to expand the NFL in the first place.

Would the talent be able to keep up? The current rosters are looking very thin in talent, the coaching staffs are
in a constant state of flux. Would many players play more then the current brutal 16 games? Washington star Corner
Josh Newman has already stated that he wouldn't play a 17th game unless they got rid of the pre-season. Does this
force players and coaches into situations where they aren't prepared for? How much furter would this give an
adavantage to well structered and run teams like the Patriots and Saints. How many of these teams would end up
being like Cleaveland? Being poorly run and having high turn over rates at the coach postions.
